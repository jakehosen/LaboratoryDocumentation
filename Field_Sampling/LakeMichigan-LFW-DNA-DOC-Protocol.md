# Lake Michigan Microbial and DOC Sampling
## Supplies for each site
* 6 — one-liter HDPE sampling bottles (can be re-used between sites)
* 1 — Sterivex PES Filter - [SVGPL10RC](https://www.sigmaaldrich.com/US/en/product/mm/svgpl10rc)
* 2 — 60 mL HDPE sampling bottles
* 1 — Luer Plug
* Pack of hematoseal
* One or two liter graduated cylinder
* Carboy of ultrapure water
* Label tape or pre-printed labels
* peristaltic pump and/or 60 mL syringes for filtering
* whirl-pak bags for storing DNA samples
* Ziploc Bag
* Dry Ice Cooler
* Regular Ice Cooler

[Some info on the Lake Guardian Rosette](https://www.epa.gov/system/files/documents/2021-12/lg200.v09-rosette-sampling_rfa.pdf)

## Lab Preparation
* Make kits with one sterivex filter, two 60 mL HDPE bottles, and a whirl-pak bag inside in a ziploc bag.
* We need 27 kits (plus you want to make some extras): There are three sampling locations, each with three sample sites in a transect from nearshore to offshore. We will collect samples at 3 different depths based on the lake profile.

## Field Sampling
* For each site we are collecting three samples.
  * The first is a shallow sample 2-5 m is preferred (shallower is better).
  * The second is the deep chlorophyll layer (DCL, also referred to by some as deep chlorophyll maximum/DCM or FMAX). If there is no DCM, collect at the middle of the metalimnion. If there is no strongly defined metalimnion, still try to get something near the largest temperature transition.
  * The third is a deep sample around 20 m. If the site is less than 20 m, get the deepest sample you can get.
* Label each sampling depth at a given site on two of the one liter sampling bottles.
* Collect the water samples from the appropriate Niskin bottles (those are the depth stratified samples taken from the Rosette). Be sure to use a small amount of water to triple rinse each bottle before filling.
* Put the peristaltic pump tube into one of the water samples and rinse about 200 mL of sample water through before filtering.
* Attach the Sterivex filter and begin filling the bottles, triple rinse each bottle with a small amount of sample. Keep track of the amount of water being filtered by putting filtrate into the graduate cylinder. The two sample bottles are 60 mL each so you can just add that to the total.
* Make sure one of the bottles is labeled DOC (Fresh) and another is labeled Nutrients (Frozen).
* Place both samples in the refrigerator. The DOC sample will be stay in the refrigerator and processed in the lab within two weeks, the nutrient sample will be frozen upon returning to the lab.
* Continuing filtering water through the Sterivex filter until all the water is used up or the filter is clogged.
* Once filtering is complete, remove the peristaltic pump tubing from the water and continue to run the pump to remove excess water from the filter. If this is not working with the peristaltic pump, use a syringe to blow air through the filter.
* After you have emptied the filter, cap one end with hematoseal and the other with a luer plug.
* Label the filter with the Site, Location, Depth, Date, and Time and include "DNA". Place the filter in a whirl-pak bag and place on dry ice.
* Upon completing filtering at a sampling location (but not in between depths) rinse the tubing with 500 mL of ultrapure water. Leave the tubing hanging to dry overnight if possible.
